Board Policy Updates

School Board Meetings, held 2-3 times a month on Tuesdays or Thursdays, discuss essential changes and enhancements to the school policies that are in or will be in place.

Recent School Board Policy updates include:

  • New initiative approved to attract students back to OCPS: As K-12 education becomes more competitive with the expansion of school choice options, we approved a partnership to help inform families about the opportunities available in their local public schools. Under the agreement, OCPS will receive approximately $8,950 in state funding for each student who re-enrolls. The third-party partner will be paid $935 per student who returns and attends for at least 30 days. This results in a net gain of $8,015 per student for the district.
  • Approving a priority access to affordable housing agreement: Orange County Public Schools has a critical need to attract and to maintain essential school district personnel such as teachers, bus drivers and other support staff. Under the new agreement, downtown Orlando area apartment complexes will prioritize OCPS employee access to available housing units. The hope is to expand this program to more properties in the future. Read the agreement here.
  • Launched A New Paid Maternity Leave Pilot Program: The district, the Orange County Classroom Teachers Association (OCCTA), and the Orange Education Support Professionals Association (OESPA) are excited to announce a significant step forward in supporting our employees and their families. Effective July 1, 2025, we will launch a Paid Maternity Leave Pilot Program for the 2025-2026 year for all employees. Eligible employees will receive three (3) weeks (15 consecutive duty days) of paid maternity leave, starting on the first duty day after the birth of their child. This pilot program is an important first step, and we will continue exploring parental leave options for potential future implementation.

The School Board is optimistic about the progress we are making to promote student success, care for our employees and staff, and elevate the quality of education in Orange County. If you have a policy recommendation, please reach out to me.

Innovation High, Luminary Middle Officially Dedicated to Lake Nona Community

Two schools have arrived in District 2! OCPS has officially welcomed Innovation High School and Luminary Middle School, expanding educational opportunities in Lake Nona.

Innovation High School, spanning 400,000 square feet across nearly 65 acres, alleviates overcrowding at both Colonial and Lake Nona High School. It offers 32 varsity and sub-varsity sports and a rich variety of specialized career pathways like Coast Guard JROTC, Agriscience, Biomedical Sciences, Culinary Sciences, Performing Arts, Engineering, and Entrepreneurship!

Luminary Middle School, covering 18 acres of land, houses 1,215 students. Offering unique electives, like Student Assistant and Media Production & Yearbook, Luminary emphasizes the role of community service and leadership development. Luminary Middle School also offers advanced STEM programs, including hands-on projects in robotics and coding, to engage students in critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

Fellows Launch D2 Math Circle, D2 TREPS to Promote Math & Entrepreneurship

The District 2 Fellows have been hard at work to promote student success across our region, which has created two very exciting opportunities that students can get involved in this summer.

District 2 Math Circle

District 2 Math Circle, in coordination with School Board Member Maria Salamanca, is organizing a FREE Competitive Math Boot Camp for current 5th-8th graders (incoming 6th-9th graders) taking place June 23 - June 26 at Lake Nona Middle School.

The aim of this boot camp is to enhance students' problem-solving skills, grow their understanding of mathematical concepts such as algebra and probability, and prepare them for math competitions such as Mathcounts. This is an incredibly valuable experience for students.

If you are interested in registering your eligible student, please visit https://tinyurl.com/D2Mathsignup.

District 2 TREPS

Callout to all rising freshmen, sophomores, juniors, and seniors! Join School Board Member Maria Salamanca and the District 2 Fellows for an entrepreneurship bootcamp and pitch competition.

Turn your business idea into reality with D2 TREPS! Over 7-weeks, you will be able to reverse engineer a successful business, gain insightful connections, develop your own model, and pitch it to investors for the opportunity to win up to $2K in seed funding investments for your business plan.

You will be working closely with 10 to 15 entrepreneurs in teams of 2 to 3 students. This program is applicable to rising freshman, sophomores, juniors, and seniors in District 2 high schools (Lake Nona, Innovation, & Colonial). Apply now at https://tinyurl.com/APPLYD2-TREPS by May 26th, 2025.

These two programs represent my commitment to elevating student success and is a testament to the leadership of my student Fellows. If you have any questions about getting involved in either of these programs, please reach out to dariel.cruzrodriguez@ocps.net.

Closing Message

As a parting message, I wanted to share some updates about the long anticipated Site 97 project. Site 97, a relief elementary school in the Storey Park area, will open in August 2026.

At a recent community meeting, community members were given two options regarding the opening timeline for Site 97. Option 1 is to plan for a normal school opening in August 2026, where Students attend their current school for an additional year, while Option 2 proposes opening the school in August 2025 in portables on the Sun Blaze campus, then to move to the new facility over spring break in March 2026.

I recommended we move forward with Option 1 for opening Site 97—and I’m proud to say that both parents and teachers strongly supported this decision. Here’s why I believe Option 1 is the best path forward:

  • Opening the school in August 2026 follows our normal school opening process. I firmly believe this minimizes disruptions to our students and teachers.
  • At the cost of postponing opening slightly, we are able to afford teachers and other staff selected for the school valuable planning time to prepare school and classrooms for students
  • Our community’s strong endorsement—from both the engaged parents and dedicated teachers at the meeting—reinforces that this decision is in the best interest of everyone. With construction on schedule (with grading, foundation work, and underground utilities already underway), we’re well positioned for a successful opening in 2026.

I have tried my best to keep the community as informed as possible every step of the way, and I am excited to welcome students and families in August 2026 when Site 97 finally opens.
